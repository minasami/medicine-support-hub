# Medicine Support Hub Visual Identity Kit

## Brand idea

Medicine Support Hub represents connected medicine access: healthcare, coordination, trust, and intelligent infrastructure working together around the patient.

## Logo concept

The logo combines:

- a central healthcare cross,
- a circular hub,
- four connected network nodes,
- a stable dark foundation.

The symbol is intentionally simple enough to work as a favicon, app icon, GitHub avatar, LinkedIn company logo, presentation mark, and product navigation icon.

## Typography

Primary digital typeface: **Inter**.

Recommended hierarchy:

- Display: Inter Bold, 48–64 px
- Page title: Inter Bold, 36–48 px
- Section heading: Inter Bold, 28–36 px
- Card heading: Inter Semibold, 18–22 px
- Body: Inter Regular, 16–18 px
- Caption: Inter Medium, 12–14 px

For Arabic interfaces, use a modern, highly legible Arabic sans-serif with visual weight compatible with Inter.

## Brand voice

Medicine Support Hub should sound:

- clear,
- credible,
- human,
- evidence-aware,
- practical,
- ambitious without exaggeration.

Avoid unsupported claims, inflated global impact figures, overly clinical language, and AI language that implies replacement of healthcare professionals.

## Messaging system

Primary descriptor:

> Digital Health Infrastructure for Medicine Access

Primary platform statement:

> One operating platform from medicine request to measurable impact.

Mission statement:

> Help organizations deliver more timely, transparent, and effective medicine support.

## Logo usage

- Maintain clear space equal to at least one network-node diameter around the mark.
- Use the full horizontal logo when space allows.
- Use the mark alone for compact interfaces and avatars.
- Use the logo on white, cloud, or infrastructure-navy backgrounds.
- Do not stretch, rotate, recolor arbitrarily, add shadows, or place over visually noisy imagery.

## Photography direction

Use authentic, respectful imagery showing:

- healthcare teams collaborating,
- pharmacists and medicine-support operations,
- community and NGO settings,
- people receiving coordinated care,
- logistics, procurement, and digital workflows.

Avoid staged stock imagery that depicts patients as helpless or reduces medicine access to generic hospital scenes.

## Illustration and iconography

Use simple geometric icons with rounded corners and consistent stroke weight. Diagrams should emphasize connection, workflow, transparency, and measurable impact.

## Social and presentation templates

Recommended recurring formats:

1. Product update card
2. Medicine-access insight card
3. Partnership announcement
4. Research finding
5. Founder perspective
6. Platform workflow diagram
7. Pilot case study
8. Event or webinar announcement

## Accessibility

- Maintain WCAG AA contrast for body text and controls.
- Never communicate status by color alone.
- Provide text alternatives for meaningful images.
- Preserve readable type sizes and generous spacing.
- Test both English and Arabic layouts.
